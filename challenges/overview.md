# Research Challenges

Ten challenges for teams of 2-4 participants working over two days (April 13-14). Each challenge has two parts:

- **Part A (Empirical) -- REQUIRED:** Data analysis using the AMC datasets. This is the core deliverable.
- **Part B (AI Governance Extension) -- OPTIONAL:** Draw analogies between your empirical findings and current AI governance debates. Explain your reasoning.

Teams choose **one challenge** and produce **one output** in their preferred format: data visualisation, 1-page policy brief, or blog post. Aim for substance over polish.

## Complex Challenges (1-7)

These involve more quantitative depth: index construction, regression, time-series analysis, or statistical comparison.

| # | Challenge | Key Question |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | [Verification Intensity by Weapon Type](01_verification_intensity.md) | Do higher-risk weapons get more verification? |
| 2 | [Does Verification Deter Participation?](02_verification_participation.md) | Does strong oversight drive states away or build confidence? |
| 3 | [Evolution of Verification Over Time](03_evolution_over_time.md) | Has verification become more sophisticated over 80 years? |
| 4 | [The Nuclear Governance Model](04_nuclear_governance.md) | Do nuclear agreements follow a distinct design template? |
| 5 | [Dual-Use Technology Coverage](05_dual_use.md) | How do treaties handle technologies with civilian and military uses? |
| 6 | [Trigger Mechanisms](06_trigger_mechanisms.md) | How are compliance mechanisms activated? |
| 7 | [The Ratification Gap](07_ratification_gap.md) | What predicts whether states follow through on commitments? |

## Simpler Challenges (8-10)
